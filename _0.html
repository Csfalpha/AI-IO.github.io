Abstract

Inertial Odometry (IO) has gained significant attention in quadrotor applications due to its lightweight design, low cost, and robust performance. However, most existing learning-based IO systems for quadrotors either use only IMU data or include additional dynamics-related inputs without a principled formulation of the underlying physical model. This lack of interpretability hampers model's ability to generalize and often limits its accuracy. In this work, we approach inertial odometry learning from a different perspective. Inspired by aerodynamic model and IMU measurement model, we identify rotor speed as a critical physical quantity required for inertial odometry and design a transformer-based architecture—the first to be applied in quadrotor IO—that exploits temporal dependencies more effectively. By incorporating rotor speed measurements, our model improves velocity prediction accuracy by 36.9% on existing methods. Furthermore, we provide a real-world quadrotor flight dataset capturing IMU measurements and rotor speed for high-speed motion. Finally, combined with an uncertainty-aware extended Kalman filter (EKF), our framework achieves superior accuracy, generalization, and real-time performance on multiple datasets and real-world systems.

Key Contributions

  • Novel Aerodynamics-Inspired Perspective — Derive physical model from quadrotor aerodynamics, offering physics-based explanation of estimation errors and validating rotor speed as a critical measurement
  • AI-IO Framework — Design a lightweight transformer-based velocity estimator coupled with an uncertainty-aware EKF, enabling real-time, high-precision pose estimation
  • Comprehensive Dataset — Establish a highly maneuverable quadrotor dataset using a self-built platform, filling a critical gap in datasets for agile quadrotor motions

Method Overview

Our approach combines aerodynamics-inspired physics modeling with transformer-based learning and uncertainty-aware Extended Kalman Filter for robust real-time quadrotor state estimation.
